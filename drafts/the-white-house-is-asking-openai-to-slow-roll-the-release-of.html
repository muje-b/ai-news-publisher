The White House is asking OpenAI to slow roll the release of its new model over safety concerns

The White House has reportedly asked OpenAI to limit the public release of its forthcoming model, GPT 5.6, citing safety worries. Instead of a wide launch, the company will share the system with a handful of trusted partners first. This move comes after direct communication from the Trump administration urging caution.

What You Need to Know

OpenAI informed selected partners that access to GPT 5.6 will be granted on a restricted basis. The decision follows a request from senior officials in the White House who expressed concerns about potential misuse of the model’s capabilities. The partners involved are said to include firms working on defense, healthcare, and education applications, though exact names have not been disclosed.

The restriction is not a permanent ban; OpenAI plans to evaluate the model’s performance and safety feedback before considering a broader rollout. Internal testing has shown that GPT 5.6 improves language understanding and generation over its predecessor, but also raises new questions about bias and content generation risks.

Officials have not released the specific safety concerns that prompted the request, but they referenced worries about deepfake‑style text, automated disinformation, and the potential for the model to be used in sensitive decision‑making tools without adequate oversight.

Why It Matters

This intervention marks one of the first times a U.S. administration has directly influenced the release schedule of a major AI model. It highlights growing governmental scrutiny of advanced AI systems and the tension between innovation speed and risk management.

For developers and businesses relying on OpenAI’s API, the delayed access could affect product timelines and competitive positioning. It also signals to other AI firms that federal oversight may become a regular factor in deployment strategies.

Key Details

  • Model name: GPT 5.6, the next iteration after GPT 5
  • Release approach: Limited to a select group of partners, not the general public
  • Reason for restriction: Safety concerns raised by the Trump administration
  • Partner sectors: Defense, healthcare, and education (specific firms unnamed)
  • Evaluation plan: OpenAI will monitor performance and feedback before a wider launch
  • Potential risks cited: Automated disinformation, bias amplification, misuse in high‑stakes decisions

What's Next

OpenAI will continue to work with the chosen partners to gather data on how GPT 5.6 behaves in real‑world scenarios. After a set review period, the company expects to decide whether to expand access, implement additional safeguards, or adjust the model’s architecture based on the findings.
