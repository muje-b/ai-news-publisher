Photoshop and Premiere now have AI assistants

Adobe is moving forward with its plan to place AI assistants inside every major Creative Cloud application. Starting today, a public beta introduces a dedicated chatbot for Photoshop, Premiere Pro, Illustrator, InDesign, and Frame.io. The assistants appear in a side panel and accept natural‑language or voice commands to perform common editing tasks, aiming to let users work faster without leaving their familiar workspaces.

What You Need to Know

The Photoshop assistant can interpret requests such as “select the subject, refine edge, and apply a subtle vignette” and then execute the corresponding tools automatically. In Premiere Pro, users can say “trim the clip to the first beat, add a cross‑fade, and export an H.264 proxy at 1080p” and the assistant will carry out the sequence on the timeline. Illustrator’s bot helps with path cleanup, color‑theme generation, and exporting assets for multiple screen sizes, while InDesign’s version assists with text‑frame balancing, style application, and PDF preset selection. Frame.io’s assistant focuses on review workflows, allowing users to tag comments, set approval statuses, and generate summary reports through simple prompts.

All assistants are powered by a fine‑tuned version of Adobe’s Firefly model, trained on each application’s specific toolset and common user actions. Input can be typed or spoken, and the panel shows a short preview of the intended action before it runs, giving users a chance to confirm or adjust. The feature is currently available to customers on the Creative Cloud All Apps plan who opt into the beta; no additional license is required beyond the existing subscription.

Adobe emphasizes that the assistants operate with user data staying on the local machine or encrypted in Adobe’s cloud, and that telemetry is optional. Users can disable the assistant at any time from the Preferences menu.

Why It Matters

By turning repetitive, multi‑step procedures into single spoken or typed commands, the assistants can reduce the time spent on routine tasks such as selections, color corrections, or export settings. This allows designers and editors to allocate more attention to creative decisions rather than memorizing menu paths or keyboard shortcuts. For newcomers, the natural‑language interface lowers the barrier to accessing advanced features that might otherwise remain hidden behind complex dialogs.

For teams, the Frame.io assistant streamlines the review cycle by automating comment tagging and status updates, which can cut down on back‑and‑forth emails. Because the assistants are context‑aware—recognizing active layers, timeline markers, or selected text—they adapt their suggestions to the current project, making the assistance feel less like a generic chatbot and more like an integrated co‑pilot.

Key Details

  • Public beta launch: Available today for Photoshop, Premiere Pro, Illustrator, InDesign, and Frame.io.
  • Model base: Each assistant uses a Firefly variant fine‑tuned to the specific app’s toolset.
  • Input methods: Supports typed commands, voice input, and optional confirmation previews.
  • Multi‑step execution: Can chain actions (e.g., “remove background, add drop shadow, export PNG 2×”) in a single request.
  • Context awareness: Reads active selections, layers, timeline markers, or text frames to tailor suggestions.
  • Data handling: Processes locally or via encrypted cloud; telemetry is opt‑in and can be disabled in Preferences.

What's Next

Adobe plans to gather feedback from the beta cohort over the coming weeks, refining the assistants’ accuracy and expanding the command vocabulary. If the response is positive, the feature will move out of beta and be rolled out to the broader Creative Cloud subscriber base, with subsequent additions planned for Lightroom, After Effects, and Adobe Express. The company also hints at deeper integration with its cloud services, such as automatic asset syncing and version‑history tracking, though exact timelines and pricing adjustments have not been disclosed.
